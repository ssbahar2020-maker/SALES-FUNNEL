What You Will Find Inside The S-Corp Tax Savings Playbook

Nine focused chapters that build your understanding step by step.

01

Understanding S-Corp Taxation

You will learn how the S-Corporation tax structure works at the federal level and why it differs from other business entities. This chapter lays the foundation for every tax decision you will make as an S-Corp owner.

02

LLC vs S-Corp Tax Treatment

This chapter breaks down the key tax differences between operating as an LLC and electing S-Corp status. You will see how each structure is taxed and what changes when you make the switch.

03

Salary, Distribution, and Reasonable Compensation

You will understand why the IRS requires S-Corp owner-employees to pay themselves a reasonable salary and how distributions are treated differently than wages. This chapter explains the relationship between these two payment types.

04

Common Business Deductions

This chapter walks through the categories of expenses that business owners commonly deduct and what documentation is generally required to support each one. You will gain a clearer picture of what qualifies as an ordinary and necessary business expense.

05

Payroll and Compliance Basics

You will learn what payroll obligations come with S-Corp ownership, including the basic filing and deposit requirements that apply to owner-employees. This chapter demystifies payroll compliance so you know what questions to ask.

06

Bookkeeping Records You Must Keep

This chapter outlines the types of financial records every S-Corp owner should maintain throughout the year and why organized books make a significant difference at tax time.

07

Year-End Tax Planning Checklist

You will discover the key actions and decisions that should be reviewed before December 31 to make sure you are positioned well for your annual filing. This chapter gives you a structured framework to approach year-end with clarity.

08

Common Mistakes and Red Flags

This chapter covers the most frequent errors S-Corp owners make on their returns and the patterns that can attract unwanted attention from the IRS. Understanding these pitfalls helps you have more informed conversations with your tax professional.

09

When to Work With a Tax Professional

You will learn which situations call for professional guidance and what to look for when choosing a qualified tax preparer or advisor for your business. This chapter helps you become a more prepared and effective client.
